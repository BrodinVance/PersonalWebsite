A structured self-study track covering the mathematical and physical foundations of quantum mechanics. 16 weeks, three textbooks, notes published here as I go.

## Texts

- **Griffiths** — *Introduction to Quantum Mechanics* (primary)
- **Axler** — *Linear Algebra Done Right* (formalism)
- **Boas** — *Mathematical Methods in the Physical Sciences* (supporting)

## Why public notes

Leaving the gaps visible is the point. It would be easy to write polished summaries after the fact. What's harder — and more useful, I think — is writing notes at the moment of confusion, before it resolves. That's when the gaps are most readable.

## Current position

Chapter 3 of Griffiths (Formalism), Chapter 5 of Axler (Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors).

The two books are converging on the same ideas from different directions, which is clarifying. Griffiths builds toward quantum formalism empirically; Axler builds toward it structurally. Seeing both simultaneously makes the structure more visible.
